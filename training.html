Service Training: Five Star Service to strengthen performance!

A six module training program, the first five modules target training team members on the attitude and skills required to consistently deliver quality service that differentiates one establishment from another. The sixth module is designed to engage the establishment’s leadership team in maintaining and leading the team to exceed customer expectations. Look for long term achievements from implementing this program as well as the ability to improve short term objectives in service.

Implementation involves a one day workshop with the management team to prepare for conducting the five modules targeted for service team members. Modules 1 to 5 can be flexibly scheduled and conducted over a two day, or longer, period of time. Team training is focused on key competencies to deliver Five Star Service and is evaluated by supervisors prior to certification.

Company Internships & School Externships

Establishing and outlining an effective path for developing people internally for career growth, or, having a plan to attract and recruit new talent is critical in today’s economical climate. How a graduate of a hospitality school views a company can start with an effective summer internship plan that demonstrates a companies commitment to individual career growth. Graduates do not want to the same position without seeing the opportunity to grow, take on new challenges and achieve greater career success.

If you are a company you need a plan for internal promotion that supports strategic growth and is viewed positively by employees. When recruiting outside the company, this plan can be critical to attracting new, dynamic talent.

To a hospitality educational program, externships are a part of the learning process where students link theory to practical application. It can be viewed as necessary credit to graduate or it can be more dynamic by linking students to career choices that best suit their talent.

Five Star Service Modules

Module 1: You are Five Star Service

Module one explains the importance of YOU and your service role in creating a positive guest experience.This module introduces the five traits one must have to meet basic guest needs. Learn how to make deposits, to the guest’s ”hospitality bank account”, which generates long-term guest loyalty.

Module 2: How to Give Quality Service

Module two explains five common guest expectations and the actions you must take to make a positive impression on the guest. You will learn how the three ways we communicate impact the guest, the importance of using names, and guidelines for telephone courtesy.

Module 3: Avoiding Sins of Service

Module three lists behaviors, actions, and situations that must be avoided. These sins are the enemies of quality of guest service and will leave guests with negative feelings toward the resort. If we commit these sins, the impact can be long term.

Module 4: Anticipate and Handle Service Problems

Module four reviews what to do when a mistake is made or when an unforeseen error occurs. How such a situation is handled can be the difference between a service sin and creating a legendary experience. Hopefully, you will never have to use the skills learned in this module. However, true service professionals know how to use proper listening skills and how to respond to criticism.

Module 5: You and the Team

Module five introduces what you can do to prepare yourself to handle the daily challenges of your service role. Because YOU make a difference, how well you take care of yourself impacts what our guests see. How you interact with team members, how you handle stress, and how you have fun reflect your individual personality. You own your personality, and this module explains how to make the most of it through a positive attitude.

Following training, participants practice the training program’s skills in actual service roles. When you demonstrate a skill on the job, it will be checked off, and once all skills have been demonstrated you will become a certified Five Star Service professional. A supervisor, manager, or trainer will sign-off each skill and when you are certified you will receive the recognition awarded service team members reaching this level of competency

Module 6: Service Leadership Session Objectives

After completing this module, managers will be able to:


  • Certify the competence of staff to deliver Five Star Service
  • List five requirements for managing a Five Star Service team
  • List three interview questions that will help you find service fanatics
  • Describe three things that will create an environment where service thrives
  • List three things each team member is empowered to do to create a Five Star Service Experience
  • List four questions to regularly ask team members regarding service
  • List the top five things a manager does to stay guest focused
  • List five things to recognize and for which to reward top service performers
  • List five things to do to have fun at work
  • Define emotional intelligence
  • List two ways emotional intelligence positively impacts performance
